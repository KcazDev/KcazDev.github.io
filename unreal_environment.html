Unreal 3D Environment

A cinematic forest environment built in Unreal Engine 5, featuring advanced lighting, physically based rendering materials, and a rich atmosphere. Set deep in a forest, the scene tells a story through its environment — a peaceful cabin a waterfall and something hiding far more dangerous lurking in the cave nearby.

Unreal Engine 5 Advanced Lighting PBR Materials Level Design Fab Store Assets Environment Art

About the Project

This project was built to develop my skills in Unreal Engine 5's environment and lighting systems. The goal was to create a scene that felt believable and atmospheric — not just a collection of assets, but a place that tells a story. Every element was placed intentionally to guide the viewer's eye through the environment and build a sense of tension.

The scene features a dense forest setting with a flowing waterfall, a worn-down cabin, a dark cave entrance, and a monster emerging from the shadows. Assets were sourced from the Fab Store and carefully combined to create a cohesive world. The real challenge was making everything feel like it belonged together — matching scale, wear, and lighting across different asset packs.

Lighting & Materials

The lighting was built using Unreal Engine 5's advanced lighting tools including Lumen for dynamic global illumination and reflections. The forest canopy filters sunlight through the trees creating natural dappled shadows on the ground, while the cave interior uses contrast between dark shadow and the cool light spilling in from outside to create a sense of depth and dread.

All surfaces use physically based rendering (PBR) materials, meaning the way light interacts with each surface — the wet rocks near the waterfall, the mossy cabin wood, the rough cave stone — behaves realistically based on real-world material properties like roughness and metallicity. This gives the scene a polished, film-quality look.
